- It's about resolving conflict - ...in a fun, respectful way - ...where everyone can play and decide
### Team Conflict? ---


  • Difference of opinion
    •

    •

    •

### Have a Throwdown! ---


  • Open, transparent format
    •
  • Anyone can contribute or observe
    •
  • Facilitated and moderated debate
    •
  • All affected can vote
    •
  • Decision is final
    •
### Throwdown Format! ---


  • Phase I: Topic Discovery
    •
  • Phase II: Debate each topic
    •
### Phase I: Topic Discovery ---


  • Everyone writes topics in "vs" format (e.g. Estimates: story points VS T-shirt sizes)
    •
  • Dot-vote all topics to determine order
    •
### Phase II: Throwdown! ---
  • Pick most popular topic
    •
  • Everyone is muted
    •
  • Two people are unmuted and to start debate
    •
  • Timebox starts
    •
  • Anyone can "tap out" someone to join conversation
    •
  • Tapped-out person is muted
    •
  • Repeat debate/tap-out process until timebox expires
    •
  • Pick next topic
    •
### You are the first! ---
Note: this has never been attempted in a virtual environment. You are about to embark on a journey that has never been attepmted. However, the rewards can be great!
### Other Rules ---
- Be kind! - Use active listening - No personal attacks - Only consider topics that you are empowered to change - Come to a throwdown with a curious mindset
### Some example topics ---
- Estimates: Story Points VS. T-Shirt Sizes - Refinement sessions: required per sprint VS not? - Product owner signoff mid sprint: required as DoD VS not? - Mid sprint defects: logging as bug VS just fixing - Tech debt: product backlog VS sprint backlog - Cloud: Azure VS AWS VS Google Cloud - Support requests: Chat VS Email VS Formal ticketing system - Project tracking: Jira VS monday.com VS Trello
