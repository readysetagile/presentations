### Early Agile Journey --- ![extreme-programming](lib/img/global/Extreme_Programming.svg)
Pair Pressure – Positive encouragement = not wanting to let partner down + satisfaction of working harder and smarter than you would alone

Pair Negotiation – Combining two sets of skills for design and implementation to yield the best solution that fits

Pair Courage – Advantage of not being alone in a challenging task, trying together what you might not alone

Pair Review – Continuous review of design and implementation that happens in context and is more likely to be acted upon

Pair Debugging – Going from problem identification to fix in a more enjoyable and often far shorter way than an individual might do it

Pair Learning – Acquiring and/or improving skills while delivering value through working with another

Pair Trust – Becoming more confident in each other’s skills and communication to present the best ideas because they feel supported

### Change Isn't Easy! --- The Satir Change Model ![satir-model](lib/img/global/satir-change-model.svg)
Virginia Satir: was an American author and therapist known as the "Mother of Family Therapy", but also for her pioneering work in the field of family reconstruction therapy. Agents of change like ourselves embrace this model to define how change impacts organizations
